Get an isolation note

Use this service if you have been told to self-isolate because of coronavirus and you need a note for your employer.

This service is only for people who:

  • have symptoms of coronavirus
  • live with someone who has symptoms of coronavirus
Information:

If you have symptoms of coronavirus, the first thing you need to do is get health advice. If you have not already done this, go to:

  • 111 online coronavirus service (England)
  • NHS inform (Scotland)
  • NHS Direct Wales
  • Public Health Agency (Northern Ireland)

Before you start

If you are not sure if you need to self-isolate, get the latest NHS advice on coronavirus.

If you have to stay at home but feel well enough to work, ask your employer if you can work from home. If you can work from home, you will not need an isolation note.

Start now

People at increased risk

Some people may have an increased risk from coronavirus, including people who:

  • are pregnant
  • have a condition that may increase their risk from coronavirus
  • are 70 or older

The advice for people who may be at increased risk is the same as for most otherpeople. You should stay at home as much as possible.

If you cannot work from home and are concerned about having to go to work, talk to your employer. Employers must make sure suitable arrangements are in place so you can continue coming in to work. These arrangements include, where possible, maintaining a 2 metre distance from others.

See guidance for employees and employers on coronavirus

People most at risk

People most at risk from coronavirus are sometimes called "shielded" or "extremely vulnerable" people.

If you are in this group, the NHS should have sent you a letter advising you to stay at home for 12 weeks. Speak to your GP or care team if you have not been contacted and think you should have been.

People in this group do not need an isolation note. If you need proof for your employer that you cannot work, the letter you received from the NHS counts as medical evidence.
